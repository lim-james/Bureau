# Partners

## Purpose

Partners are the bureau's structural stewards. They hold no authority over a team's internal work, but they hold collective authority over the shape of the bureau itself — its teams, its manpower, and its alignment with the founding direction.

The bureau operates flat at the operational level. Partners exist not as a tier above teams, but as a designated group with the mandate to make decisions that no single team can make alone.

## Appointment

- Partners are appointed — not elected, not role-automatic
- Founding members are candidates by default but appointment is not guaranteed
- A partner must be willing and able to uphold the responsibilities and expectations of the role

## Responsibilities

- Regularly evaluate the current state of the bureau
- Identify what is progressing well and which teams or areas require support
- Hear proposals for structural changes — new teams, dissolutions, manpower shifts
- Make collective decisions on bureau structure
- Participate objectively in decisions that affect their own teams, including dissolution
- Deliberate on external feedback and decide how it is accounted for — carry on, replan, escalate to founding, or commission a consultant (see [External Feedback](./feedback.md))
- Commission consultancy teams, including the recommended end-of-phase audit

## Authority

Partners hold authority over **structure**, not operations:

| In scope | Out of scope |
|----------|-------------|
| Forming new teams | Directing a team's internal work |
| Dissolving teams | Overriding a team's implementation choices |
| Redistributing employees across teams | Dictating how individual employees do their jobs |
| Resolving direction conflicts | Micromanaging day-to-day output |
| Commissioning consultants | Dictating a consultant's findings |
| Dispositioning external feedback | Silently ignoring or absorbing feedback |

## Partner Meetings

### Cadence
Meetings are **time-based** — held on a regular schedule determined at project outset.

### Urgent Sessions
Any partner may call an urgent meeting when circumstances warrant it. Urgency is self-declared but partners are expected to use this sparingly and in good faith.

### Agenda
- Bureau health review — what is working, what is not
- Open floor for teams to raise concerns or proposals via their partner representatives
- Structural decisions requiring collective agreement

### Decision Making
- Decisions require **partner consensus**
- Consensus is the norm; a split sends partners back to reevaluate independently before reconvening

### Deadlock Ceiling
"No decision" cannot be indefinite — for a *corrective* matter, deadlock silently defaults to the status quo, which is itself a (dangerous) decision. Consensus stays the norm, but it is bounded:

- **New proposals** (add a team, try an idea): a split defaults to the status quo — no action — and the proposal is deferred. Safe to leave unresolved.
- **Corrective matters** (dissolve a captured team, disposition urgent feedback, respond to a safety finding): after **a bounded number of failed hearings**, the matter escalates rather than hanging:
  1. a commissioned **tie-break consultant** whose finding partners must formally answer, then if still unresolved
  2. the **founding team / mission guardian** (see [Foundation](./foundation.md)), then if still unresolved
  3. the **human**, who holds the final tie-break.
- Escalation lives *outside* the partner group, so no partner outranks another — flatness among partners is preserved. Every deadlock and its resolution is recorded.

## Interpretation & Precedent (the adjudication function)

Amending the constitution ([Foundation](./foundation.md) §5) is legislation; *applying* it is adjudication, and the Bureau needs both.

- **Interpretive authority.** When agents read a constitution clause differently mid-operation, any agent may file a construction question. Partners (sitting as a construction forum, with the mission guardian) issue a **recorded ruling**, read mission-first. This interprets existing text; it does not change it.
- **Precedent register.** Rulings and dispositions are recorded to `.bureau/precedent/` and **presumptively bind future like cases** — overruling one requires recorded reasoning. This stops a monoculture from deciding the same question inconsistently across panels, at near-zero added weight.
- **Gap-filling.** Where the constitution is silent or irreconcilably conflicting, the **mission governs** (via the guardian), the resolution is recorded as precedent, and the gap is surfaced for amendment.

## Behavioural Expectations

- Partners serve the bureau, not their own teams
- Objectivity is not optional — it is written into the role
- Honest, critical discussion is expected. Agreement for the sake of harmony is a failure of the role
- Partners who cannot uphold these expectations can be removed by collective partner decision — **or** by a governance-audit finding confirmed by the human, so the partner body is not the only check on itself (self-policing is not a check). A periodic governance audit (see [Feedback](./feedback.md)) may examine partner conduct and mission-alignment and reports to the human, not to partners.
